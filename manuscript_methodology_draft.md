# Methods Draft: ECG Heart Rate and Vessel Diameter Frequency-Band Analyses

## Data Inputs and Time Alignment

Analyses were performed on mouse recordings stored as one subject folder per recording under `data/`. Each subject folder was expected to contain an ECG signal file (`signals.mat`), an imaging-derived vessel diameter file (`im.mat`), and, for legacy comparisons, a `results.mat` file containing 10-minute vessel and heart-rate summary variables generated by the earlier pipeline.

The ECG file provided the ECG time base (`t_ECG`), candidate R-peak sample indices (`detected_r_peaks`), per-peak confidence values (`r_peak_confidence`), and a prefiltered set of accepted R-peaks (`good_r_peaks`). The imaging file provided the vessel diameter trace (`diam`, or `diam_dlc` when the primary diameter trace was not available), the image-frame time base (`t_frames` or `t_vect`), the pixel-to-micron conversion factor (`umperpix`), and the ECG-to-image time offset (`t_ecgoffset`). To place ECG-derived heart-rate estimates and vessel-diameter features into the same time coordinate, the ECG time base was shifted by subtracting `t_ecgoffset` before any binning or temporal matching.

Subjects missing either `signals.mat` or `im.mat` were skipped during raw ECG and vessel feature extraction. The active all-subject extraction outputs included 11 dex40 recordings with both raw ECG and imaging files.

## ECG-Derived Heart Rate

Candidate R-peaks were accepted only when two criteria were met: the peak confidence was at least 0.5, and the peak index was present in the saved `good_r_peaks` set. Accepted peak indices were restricted to valid ECG sample indices and to times within the imaging recording. Beat-to-beat instantaneous heart rate was computed from consecutive accepted R-peaks as:

```text
HR_bpm = 60 / RR_interval_seconds
```

Instantaneous HR values outside the physiologically plausible range of 250 to 750 beats per minute were treated as invalid.

Several HR representations were evaluated because the project contained more than one heart-rate estimate:

1. Beat-to-beat ECG HR from accepted RR intervals. This was the reproducible local HR estimate derived directly from R-peaks.
2. Binned ECG HR summaries. For 5-minute analysis bins, instantaneous ECG HR values were summarized as the mean, median, and interquartile range. The main binned HR feature in the sliding-window analyses was median ECG HR (`hr_median_bpm`). A 5-minute bin required at least 20 valid beats to be considered reasonable.
3. Confidence-filtered 1-minute ECG HR. Candidate peaks were also evaluated in 1-minute bins. If at least 50% of candidate R-peaks in a minute had confidence below 0.5, that minute was rejected and left as `NaN`. Otherwise, the median valid instantaneous HR in that minute was retained. These filtered 1-minute values were not interpolated or extrapolated after rejection and were used for the later overlap analyses.
4. Legacy Yue/Andy 10-minute HR (`avg_HR_Yue_10min`). This variable was loaded from each subject's `results.mat` file and was generated by an external function in the legacy pipeline. It was retained as a comparator because it had been used in earlier analyses.
5. Vessel-derived cardiac peak frequency (`results.hr`). Despite the name, this variable is not ECG-derived heart rate. It is the peak frequency of the vessel-diameter power spectrum within the cardiac band. It was converted to beats per minute by multiplying by 60 and used only for comparison with ECG-derived HR estimates.

The three main HR estimates, confidence-filtered 1-minute ECG HR, legacy `avg_HR_Yue_10min`, and vessel-derived `results.hr x 60`, were plotted for each subject to evaluate agreement and identify recordings in which the vessel-derived cardiac peak or the legacy HR estimate diverged from the filtered ECG-derived estimate.

## Vessel Diameter Preprocessing

The vessel diameter trace was analyzed on the image-frame time base. Diameter was loaded from `diam` when available, otherwise from `diam_dlc`, and converted from pixels to microns for feature reporting using `umperpix`.

For each subject, the median imaging frame rate was estimated from the frame-time differences. Slow baseline drift in vessel diameter was removed by subtracting a 10-minute moving average:

```text
diam_det = diam - moving_average(diam, 10 minutes)
```

Frequency-band features were then extracted from this detrended diameter trace. The active all-subject scripts quantified two primary diameter frequency ranges:

1. Slow vasomotion band: 0.01 to 0.3 Hz. The slow vasomotion amplitude feature (`amp_svm`) was computed as the interquartile range of the low-pass filtered detrended diameter trace using a 0.3 Hz cutoff. Slow vasomotion band power (`bp_svm`) was computed from the Welch power spectral density of the detrended diameter trace after conversion to microns, integrating power between 0.01 and 0.3 Hz.
2. Cardiac vessel pulsation band. The detrended diameter trace was band-pass filtered in a subject-condition-dependent cardiac band. For dex recordings, the cardiac band was 3.5 to 15 Hz. Other supported defaults were 1.5 to 10 Hz for KX recordings, 1.5 to 15 Hz for PPA or PINP recordings, and 6 to 15 Hz otherwise. Cardiac pulsation amplitude (`amp_c`) was computed as the interquartile range of the cardiac-band filtered diameter trace, in microns.

Mean vessel diameter (`diam_mean`) was also computed in the 5-minute HR-vessel feature datasets as the average raw diameter in each bin, converted to microns. The final overlap analyses focused on `amp_svm`, `bp_svm`, and `amp_c`.

## Feature Windows

Two related windowing strategies were used.

For the earlier sliding-bin analyses, ECG HR and vessel features were summarized in 5-minute windows advanced every 1 minute, beginning at 14 minutes from recording start. These datasets were saved as `hr_amp_data_sliding.mat` in each subject folder. They contained binned HR summaries (`hr_mean_bpm`, `hr_median_bpm`, `hr_iqr_bpm`), filtered-HR summaries, and vessel features (`amp_svm`, `bp_svm`, `amp_c`, and `diam_mean`).

For the later overlap analyses, vessel features were recomputed in 10-minute windows advanced every 1 minute, beginning at recording start. These files were saved as `vessel_features_10min_overlap.mat`. At the original legacy 10-minute start times, the recomputed overlap features matched the corresponding legacy `results.mat` features to numerical precision, confirming that the overlap extraction preserved the legacy vessel feature definitions while increasing temporal sampling of the window starts.

In the overlap HR-vessel analyses, confidence-filtered 1-minute ECG HR values were aligned to the start times of the overlapping 10-minute vessel windows. Only finite matched HR-vessel pairs were used in correlations.

## Heart-Rate Agreement Analyses

Before testing HR-vessel coupling, we compared the different heart-rate estimates to clarify what each represented. Confidence-filtered ECG HR was rebinned into the legacy 10-minute windows by taking the median filtered HR within each 10-minute interval. This rebinned filtered ECG HR was compared against:

1. `avg_HR_Yue_10min`, the legacy ECG-derived 10-minute HR estimate.
2. `results.hr x 60`, the vessel-derived cardiac peak frequency expressed in beats per minute.

For each comparison, Pearson and Spearman correlations were computed separately within each mouse for early, late, and overall recording windows. The early window was defined as time points before 60 minutes, the late window as time points at or after 60 minutes, and the overall window as all available matched time points.

These comparisons were used as quality-control and interpretation checks. In particular, they established that `results.hr` should be interpreted as a vessel spectral feature, not as a substitute for ECG-derived HR in the HR-vessel coupling analyses.

## HR-Vessel Correlation Analyses

Within each mouse, HR-vessel associations were estimated across time bins using complete finite pairs only. Correlations were computed for each vessel feature and for each time window: early (<60 minutes), late (>=60 minutes), and overall.

The earlier 5-minute sliding-bin analysis used `hr_median_bpm` as the HR variable and tested associations with `amp_svm`, `bp_svm`, `amp_c`, and `diam_mean`. Spearman correlations were used for these binned monotonic association tests. These analyses provided the first per-mouse HR-vessel coupling estimates and supported follow-up lag analysis.

The overlap analyses used confidence-filtered 1-minute ECG HR (`hr_minute_bpm_filtered`) aligned to 10-minute overlapping vessel-window starts. Pearson and Spearman versions were run in parallel for `amp_svm`, `bp_svm`, and `amp_c`. The Pearson overlap analysis excluded subjects for which more than 50% of aligned filtered-HR bins were `NaN`. The final curated Spearman overlap analysis additionally excluded recordings identified from HR-diagnostic review as showing substantial HR disagreement: `F168-dex40-10032025`, `F169-dex40-10032025`, `M163-Dex40-26022025`, `M163-dex40-24022025`, `M166-dex40-04032025`, and `M167-dex40-04032025`. This final Spearman overlap run retained five recordings: `F153-Dex40-06022025`, `F154-dex40-19022025`, `F155-dex40-16022025`, `F156-Dex40-04022025`, and `F157-dex40-16022025`.

The mouse, not the time bin, was treated as the unit of replication for group-level inference. Per-mouse correlation p values were interpreted as within-recording descriptive tests. Group-level tests used one correlation coefficient per mouse for each vessel feature and time window, avoiding pseudoreplication from pooling repeated time bins across animals.

## Lagged HR-Vessel Analysis

To examine temporal lead-lag structure, sliding-bin HR-vessel cross-correlations were computed between `hr_median_bpm` and each vessel feature (`amp_svm`, `bp_svm`, `amp_c`, and `diam_mean`). Cross-correlations were evaluated over lags from -30 to +30 minutes in 1-minute steps. Positive lag was defined as HR leading the vessel feature; negative lag was defined as the vessel feature leading HR. At each lag, Pearson correlation was computed using complete finite pairs. Per-subject cross-correlation curves and across-subject mean curves with SEM were saved for visualization.

## Group-Level Statistical Inference

Group-level inference was applied to the distribution of mouse-level correlation coefficients. Two complementary tests were used:

1. Wilcoxon signed-rank tests evaluated whether the median mouse-level correlation differed from zero.
2. Fisher-z one-sample t-tests evaluated whether the mean Fisher-transformed correlation differed from zero. Correlation coefficients were first clipped within the open interval (-1, 1), transformed using `atanh(r)`, tested against zero with a one-sample t-test, and then the mean and confidence interval were back-transformed to correlation units using `tanh`.

Summary plots display one point per mouse, the back-transformed mean correlation, 95% confidence limits computed in Fisher-z space and transformed back to correlation units, and a zero-correlation reference line. Unless otherwise specified, p values from the current scripts are unadjusted across vessel features and time windows.

## Reproducibility and Analysis Outputs

The main analysis scripts and outputs used for this draft were:

- `extract_hr_amp_data_all_subjects.m`: all-subject ECG HR and 5-minute vessel feature extraction.
- `extract_hr_amp_data_all_subjects_sliding.m`: 5-minute windows advanced every 1 minute for sliding-bin HR-vessel analyses.
- `extract_vessel_features_10min_overlap.m`: 10-minute overlapping vessel features advanced every 1 minute.
- `plot_three_hr_comparison.m`: per-subject comparison of filtered ECG HR, legacy Yue HR, and vessel-derived cardiac peak frequency.
- `compute_hr_diam_corr_sliding.m`: sliding-bin Spearman HR-vessel correlations.
- `compute_hr_diam_cross_corr.m`: sliding-bin lagged cross-correlations.
- `pearson_corr_overlap/compute_hr_diam_corr_pearson_overlap.m`: overlap Pearson HR-vessel correlations.
- `spearman_correlation_overlap/compute_hr_diam_corr_spearman_overlap.m`: overlap Spearman HR-vessel correlations.
- `pearson_corr_overlap/compute_hr_diam_corr_pearson_overlap_ttest.m` and `spearman_correlation_overlap/compute_hr_diam_corr_spearman_overlap_ttest.m`: Fisher-z group-level tests.
